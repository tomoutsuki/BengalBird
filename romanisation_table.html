SVOR (SONORANT) LETTERS
অ o IPA / ɔ /. As o in British English hOt. The letter is called স্বর-অ svoro-o in Bengali.

In Bengali orthography, in the absence of contra-indication and whether or not svoro-o is actually pronounced, every consonant sign by default comprises a consonant sound followed by an inherent svoro-o. Consequently, in Bengali orthography svoro-o is never actually written immediately after a consonant.

Where the difference in pronunciation between o in British English hOt and o in English Obey is to be shown, svoro-o with the pronunciation of IPA / ɔ / can be written as ó.

h Silent. The letter in Bengali orthography is silent when it occurs immediately before ঋ rwi, between a vowel and a ্য jzo-phola (the jzo-phola in this circumstance will be Romanised as jz — see rule 11), or before any (other) consonant. The pronunciation will be doubled of any such jzo-phola or other consonant (ঋ rwi is not a consonant), except at the start of a word.

If the অন্তঃস্থ হ ontohwstho ho occurs between a vowel and a ঋ rwi, the pronunciation will change of such ঋ rwi to IPA / rri / (as re-ri in English caRE-RIdden).
